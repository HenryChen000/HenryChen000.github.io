Prokofiev: Violin Concerto No. 1

Dopamine Series · 5 Aug, 2025

"reminder: First of all, the excerpts chosen in the Dopamine Series are very subjective. In the Dopamine Series, I share especially beautiful fragments or pieces of classical music with the aim of making classical music interesting to those who have never experienced it before. However, I strongly encourage you to listen to the entire work—only by experiencing the whole piece can you truly understand what the composer intended to express."

Excerpt: 6:00–7:55

One day I came across this album and was immediately struck by how memorable it was. The cover was truly distinctive—bright, colorful flowers and plants all around Hilary Hahn, so vibrant and eye-catching.

Right away I sensed there must be some deeper intention behind the cover design—perhaps an echo of the music itself. So I listened to the album, and when I reached about 6:00 into the third movement of Prokofiev: Violin Concerto, I finally understood why the cover was designed that way. Jesus, it was like stepping into a fairy-tale realm—I felt I had completely left the real world behind.
