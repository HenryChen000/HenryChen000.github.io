Symphonie fantastique

Dopamine Series · 10 May, 2026

"Note: In the Dopamine Series, I share especially beautiful fragments or pieces of classical music with the aim of making classical music interesting to those who have never experienced it before. However, I strongly encourage you to listen to the entire work—only by experiencing the whole piece can you truly understand what the composer intended to express."

Excerpt: 2nd mvt, 14:53–21:06

I first came across Symphonie fantastique because I kept seeing it on YouTube. Whether I was casually scrolling through videos or looking for classical music recordings, I noticed that so many orchestras and conductors had performed or recorded this piece. Over time, I realized that it must be a truly great symphony.

And after finally listening to it, I understood why. The beauty of the melodies, the dramatic intensity, and the sheer imagination of the music all fully deserve its legendary reputation. The entire work feels exactly like its title suggests—full of fantasy and dreamlike colors. Listening to it is genuinely a magical experience.

The second movement in particular is unforgettable. It feels magical, addictive, romantic, and sweet all at once. If you happen to have a crush on someone, I think you would absolutely love it, because it captures the feeling of being secretly in love so perfectly.
